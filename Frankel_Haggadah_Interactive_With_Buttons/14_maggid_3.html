Maggid – Part 3: Moses and the Call to Action

Moses, raised in Pharaoh’s palace but born of Hebrew blood, fled Egypt after witnessing injustice. In the wilderness, God called to him from a burning bush: “I have seen the suffering of My people... now go. I will send you to Pharaoh.” Reluctant, unsure, and human — Moses accepted the call. Liberation always begins with someone saying yes, even in fear.
We imagine heroes as fearless. But Moses hesitated. He doubted. And still, he led. Courage isn't certainty — it’s commitment.
