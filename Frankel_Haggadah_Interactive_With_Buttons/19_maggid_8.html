Maggid – Part 8: Freedom and Song

On the far shore, safe at last, the Israelites sang. Miriam picked up her tambourine, and the women danced. The Song of the Sea echoed — “Horse and rider He has hurled into the sea.” It was not just a celebration of escape, but of transformation. Slaves no longer. A free people.
Singing was their first act as free people. Joy as resistance. Art as declaration. Dance as defiance.
